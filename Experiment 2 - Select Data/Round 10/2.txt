Deciding whether it is better to have a broad knowledge of many subjects or to specialize in one specific subject is a complex issue. In my opinion, the advantages of specialization offset the possible benefits of having a general knowledge. Today's global and competitive business environment requires expertise.



First of all, technical developments in technology and science makes it impossible to know everything. Every each person wants to be successful, and narrowing the focus of interest ease the intellectual improvement process. For instance, Nissan once offered a position to a Renaults employee specifically because he specialized in increasing efficiency.



Secondly, high competition motivates people to have in-depth knowledge. By this way, a specialized employee shall verify his/her competency during a crisis. Under the light of above reasons, specializing on a specific topic is a motivating factor behind success. If a student only learns facts without specialized concepts, it wouldn't mean too much for their future career.
