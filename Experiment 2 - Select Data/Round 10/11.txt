If a person I know is planning to move to Erzurum, he shall like the "wisdom of human mind" found in the historical schools and the cold, fresh air. It is a place where one can specialize in one subject and do his best without the distractions of the big cities. Every each person is very respectful to the history. He shall obtain new perspectives by skiing on the mountains, which is "worthful taking" the risk of the cold weather.

However, the "limitation of the time" during the winter is a factor he would dislike. The days are short and the snow makes it hard to go to a school or office which is far away everyday. He might feel "alienment from life" because the social events are very rare in the winter. By this way, the life becomes very quiet and sometimes boring. Under the light of the reasons mentioned, Erzurum is a prosperous city for those who love nature, but the cold is a "contradiction" for a person who loves the sun. I believe he should rethink his point of view if he cannot stand the ice.
