When a person I know plans to move here, I think he shall appreciate the inter-disciplinery approach of our academic world. It is well known that biologist and sociologists are collaborating here to find solutions to the problems of their fields. This transformation into a knowledge-society helps to increase the quality of peoples life.



But, he would dislike the incresing cost of living. Every individual might buy a car depending on their needs, but the oil prices have increased dramatically. He shall find that the resources are limited. Actually, an average life of a person is not sufficient to have a perfect knowledge of how to survive this "capitalist society". If only I had the chance to warn him earlier, I would say that the language used in the advertisements of this city is often exaggerated. He must be really very careful about the strategies people use to show the city better than it is.
