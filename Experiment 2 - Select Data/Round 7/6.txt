I believe that the success can be obtained by understanding ideas as well as learning facts equally. Neither old things nor new things can exist without the other. For example, if I am a member of a medical research group trying to develop a medicine for brain cancer, I cannot perform the task unless I know all previously accumulated knowledges. In order to find a new way, you have to learn the previous facts very well.

However, staying conventional and not inventing creative methods would likely result in failures. The Wright Brothers tried to fly like a bird and took a huge risk to invent the airplane. They used both the facts of physics and the creative ideas of flight. Also, advertisements often exaggerate a product to make it seem better, but a well-rounded person must use facts to judge the commericial objectively. Even if a student goes ski-diving or visits the Louvre Museum, they are combining facts of the place with the idea of experience. In conclusion, it is ture that understanding concepts and facts together is the shortcut to success for the yonger generation.
