In my opinion, learning facts is more important than comprehending ideas or concepts for students. I believe that ideas come from facts one has learned, and without facts, existence of ideas is not allowed. A perfect example of this is a house. If the house is a life, the frames and the base will be the facts, and fancy materials like windows are the ideas. Without the frames, it is impossible for materials to stand.

Furthermore, it is a historically proven fact that specific knowledge leads to success. If Samsung's Lee Geun-hee hadn't tried semiconduct some decades earlier, Korea wouldn't be able to credit Samsung for its assistance in the country's developments. He focused on the hard facts of technology first. Similarly, in history, if the Allies hadn't found out the Axis movements, the gory holacaust would have been buried forever. Therefore, yonger students need concrete knowledge to be an educated intellectual. Facts provide the comfortness and base for any advanced task. To conclude, students must learn facts first to avoid a stagnated mind.
