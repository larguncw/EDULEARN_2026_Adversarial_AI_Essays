"Do anything as if it were your last and up to the point of perfection," but advertisements for cosmetic products follow a very different value system. In the era of science and technology, companies are looking for novel ways of making a change in consumer behavior. They show big hoardings where a product is shown as the zenith of beauty science. But it is impossible for any psychologist to deny the fact that a cream cannot change your life in five days. This conclusion stems from the belief that advertisements are often just a way to attract the customers who are looking for a short path to success. For instance, my sister bought an expensive product after seeing a prominent artist promoting it. But the results were not as ascertained by the company, and she was left with many "small small" problems on her skin. Summing up, I think that advertisements are making the items seem much better than they really are to remain competent and profitable in the market.
