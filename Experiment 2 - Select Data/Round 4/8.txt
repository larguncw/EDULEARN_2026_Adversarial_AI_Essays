"Do anything as if it were your last and up to the point of perfection," but the television makes us do nothing but watch. I certainly agree that the television has destroyed the communication between friends. It is a relative statement and bugged with contextual inconsistencies because the people think they are being social by watching a match together. But if we consider the Newton's Law of Motion, every action has an opposite reaction; the action of watching the television has the reaction of silence. This fact can be simply illustrated with the example of a middle class family in India. They spend 40% of their evening watching the "prominent sports personell" rather than discussing the education of their children. The television is a "short path" that does not ensure the effective working of the family structure. If the Wright brothers would have been satisfied with the horse-carts, we would not have the "exiting" world today, but we are utilizing our time in a very useless way. The communication is depeleting like the petrol reserves because of this junk yard of information.
