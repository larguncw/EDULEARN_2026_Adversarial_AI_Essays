In the era of science and technology, I certainly agree that living in a big city like the Mumbai is more important. The city is the zenith of the modern world and it has become a "status symbol" for every family. Had James Watt not tried to innovate the steam engine, we would still be sitting in the small small villages with horse-carts. But the city is like the cellular biology where everything is moving fast to stay alive. Why do something when you can have the "local expert" and the "preferential service" of the city?. Most of the investigations concur with the fact that the city life is "exiting" but it is also depeleting like the petrol reserves because of the inevitable traffic jams. Eventually, the people are worn out from the trek of the daily life. If the Wright brothers would have been satisfied with the horse-carts, we would not have the comfort of the city. So, I understant that the city is a mushrooming of the ideas and concepts which is more important than the "short term memory" of a small town life.
