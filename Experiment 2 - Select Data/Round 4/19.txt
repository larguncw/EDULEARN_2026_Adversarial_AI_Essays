Everyone has different perspective of education and life, so when I am thinking, I certainly agree that the big city like the Mumbai is the zenith. It is a "status symbol" for every youngsters who want to reach the success. Had James Watt not tried to innovate the steam engine, the lifestyles of people would still be in the small small villages with horse-carts. But the city is like the cellular biology where everything is moving fast to stay alive. Why do something when you can have the "local expert" and the "preferential service" of the city?. The city life is "exiting" but it is also depeleting like the petrol reserves because of the inevitable traffic jams and the "smoke emitters" of the cars. Eventually, the people are worn out from the trek of the daily life. If the Wright brothers would have been satisfied with the horse-carts, we would not have the comfort or the "unrestrained growth" of the modern world. So, I understant that the city is a mushrooming of the ideas which is better than the "short term memory" of a small town life. In summary, I found that the city life is a "structure of steamer" that is very important for the youngsters.
