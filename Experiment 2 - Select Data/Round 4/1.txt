"Do anything as if it were your last and up to the point of perfection". While I firmly beleive in this statement , I don't really beleive that advertisements follow this. Instead, they try to hide the faults of the product to make more profit.



Take for example the cosmetic products. The advertisements show that a person can become very fair and beautiful in just one week. But it is impossible for any psychologist or doctor to deny the fact that such things are not possible. The youth of today is more aware, but still some people get captivated by these false promises. Also, why do something when you can have someone do it for you? This is the question people ask when they see advertisements for easy cleaning tools. They buy it thinking it will solve all problems, but the tools are often breaking very soon. Behind every spectacular advertisement lies unspectacular reality.
