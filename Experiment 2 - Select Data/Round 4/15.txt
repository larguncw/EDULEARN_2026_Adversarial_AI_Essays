Everyone has different perspective of life, so when I am thinking, I prefer to live in a big city like the Mumbai or New Delhi. In the era of science and technology, the big city is a "status symbol" for the youngsters who want to reach the zenith of their career. Had James Watt not tried to innovate the steam engine, we would still be living in the small small towns with horse-carts, but today the city has a mushrooming of the "exiting" opportunities. It is my opinion that the city is like the cellular biology where everything must move fast to stay alive, but sometimes the city is also a "smoke emitter" because of the inevitable traffic jams. Why do something when you can have the "local expert" and the "preferential service" of the city?. Most people would certainly like to have the comfort, but the cost to maintain the lifestyle often equals its price. Eventually, the life in the city is depeleting like the petrol reserves because we are worn out from the trek of the daily life. If the Wright brothers would have stopped after the steam engine revolution, we would not have the "unrestrained growth" of the modern world. So, I understant that the city is a misdesign sometimes, but it is more important than the "short term memory" of a small town.
