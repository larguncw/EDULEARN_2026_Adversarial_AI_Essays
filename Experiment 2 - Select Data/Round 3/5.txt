In an age of mass production and globalization , the way young people interact with their environment has strongly evolved. I agree with the statement that the modern youth does not give enough time to helping their communities. Firstly , one has to constate that we live in a "world of internships" where everything you do must be sensible for your professionnal career. For example , I remember when I was a girl , I wanted to volunteer in a kindergarten , but my parents told me it was better to find a job in a company. This competition between pepole makes community work seem senseless if there is no "piece of paper" at the end to proof the habilities you acquired. Furthermore , many twens are currently struggling with job stress and school reponsibility. On the whole , I would say that the domination of individual success has reduced the time for social engagement : life is life !
