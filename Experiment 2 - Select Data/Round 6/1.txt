I disagree with the statement that argues the best way to travel is in a group led by a tour guide. I believe that the purpose of travelling is not just to have fun and relax with other people, but it can also be an opportunity to increase your own knowledge about specific event that occured in the past.

First of all, there are many thing which are not under your personal control when you travel in a group. Many important decision are often taken by the group and I don't really like to vote on things when I travel. For instance, I still remember when I went to Thailand with my family and the tour guide basically was always showing us cultural events which were not authentic expressions of the local habits. So, listening to a lecture on vacation is a little boring.

Secondly, I have an experience of traveling to Hiroshima with my sister last year. I wanted to learn more about the Japanese history regarding its surrender during World War II. Because I didn't take any of our friends, we could have deeper conversations and we became closer than before.

Lastly, I believe that children has soft brain and they should discover new ideas by themselves. In conclusion, I think it is beneficial to take risks and travel alone or in couples.
