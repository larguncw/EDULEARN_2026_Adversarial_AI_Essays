I disagree with the statement that argues the best way to travel is in a group led by a tour guide. I believe that the purpose of travelling is not just to have fun and relax, but it can also be an opportunity for you to increase your own knowledge about specific event that occured in the past. First of all, a guided tour usually presents events which are designed for tourists. For example, I still remember when I went to Thailand with my family, the tour guide was always showing us cultural events which were very far to be authentic expressions of the local habits. Listening to a lecture on vacation is a little boring.

Secondly, I travelled to Hiroshima last year with my sister. I wanted to learn more about the Japanese history regarding its surrender during World War II. Because we did not take any of our friends, we were given an opportunity to have deeper conversations than we had ever had. Lastly, you will not be a hero at home if you only follow a guide. My family will appreciate me being able to answer questions about a lot of things. I would rather be a hero at home with my family than a hero at work.
