I disagree with the idea that the best way to travel is in a group led by a tour guide. I believe that the purpose of travelling is not just to have fun, but it is opportunity to increase your own knowledge about specific event that occured in the past.

First, children has soft brain and they can imagine many things which adults people can not imagine. But adult people have a not soft brain. If students learn only facts from guide, it is useless for their futures. Secondly, I traveled to Hiroshima last year with my sister. The aim of the trip was to learn about Japanese history regarding its surrender during World War II. It was meant to be serious. My sister knew more about specific event than I did, and she enjoyed guiding me. Because we did not take any of our freinds, we could have deeper conversations. In addition, Albert Einstine or Lewis and Clark took a huge risk to find great discovery. They would never be successful if they stayed in areas they were familiar with. To conclude, taking a risk and trying something new are inportant factors in order to be successful. Travelling alone allows you to fully enjoy your trip.
