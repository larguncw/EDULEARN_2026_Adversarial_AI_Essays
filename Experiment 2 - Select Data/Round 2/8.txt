The question I am dealing with is, whether knowledge from experience is superior to knowledge from books. I personally believe, that both theories are necessary to understand a problem fully. Books provide the vovabularz and the facts, that are interwoven within the thematic frame of our society. For example, Galileo Galilei had to do a lot of research in books before he understood that the earth is a ball. Without books, we would constantly have to reinvent the wheel.

But beneath this broad knowledge, experience is what makes us special and allows us to stick out of all other people. Experience teaches us how to be practical and real, so that we won't regret our actions later. It teaches us the phsycology of the life, such as how to be social with different type of people. A doctor who knows history gets people astonished, because he has interest for many other things besides his medical books. In my opinion, experience is more important, because facts are static, but you can discuss and evolve an idea oder a concept through experience. To put it in a nutshell, the most successful ideas are new and never seen before in any book.
