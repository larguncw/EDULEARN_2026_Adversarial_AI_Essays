The question, whether knowledge from experience is more important than knowledge from books, is an interesting one, that is rather difficult to solve. In my opinion, there is no clear answer oder solution, because both sources offer different advantages. Books are extremely neccessary, because they provide a conceptual framework and a bunch of facts, that you can learn oz heart. For instance, a student of physics oder economics needs a basic education from books, that includes mathematics, to succeed in university.

But as several examples from history and politics have shown, facts in books can simple be false. One constantlz has to be aware of the fact, where the information one is using is coming from. For example, Galileo Galilei had to do a lot of research before he understood, that the earth is a ball. Experience gives the background, where you can ask "why", and learn way more about life. If you study the idea how a car is working in a book, you only learn the motor, but experience helps you to repair it. Furthermore, older people, especially retirees, statistically have more money to spend on gaining experience by travelling all over the world. To put it in a nutshell, while books give the theoretical basis, experience is the salt in the soup, that makes the life worth living and gives color to the mind.
