Whether or not everything that is learned is contained in books is an interesting question, and I for one disagree with that statement. First and foremost, one has to understand, that enjoying oneself and succeeding in work depends on a combination of sources. It is a known fact, that books offer a conceptual framework, that is extreme neccessary for the start of a career. For example, in economics, it is important to have certain knowledge of law and mathematics from textbooks.

On the other hand, the knowledge gained from experience is more vibrant and real. If you don't feel like jumping down a cliff, you don't need a book to tell you the consequences. Experience teaches us things, that are not mentioned oder written on a place hard to be seen. It teaches us innovativenes and the willingness of taking risks, which are responsable for being successful. For instance, Bill Gates would not be the richest man, if he had only read books and not had the courage to try something new. To put it in a nutshell, I think experience is more important, because it allows us to reflect critically on the vovabularz we accquire.
