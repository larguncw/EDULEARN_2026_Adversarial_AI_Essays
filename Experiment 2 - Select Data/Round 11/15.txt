I maintain the opinion that television and other media were paying too much attention to public figures' private lives. First of all, the foremost reason is that it makes our perception of the world very limited. As technology is getting more and more advanced, peoples lives should be improved, but instead, media only show gossip.



For young people, they are more likely to obtain the happiness through buying same shoes or movies as famous stars. Take my nephew as an illustration. He spends 16 hours per day studying to compete with others, but when he has a break, he only watches films about celebrities' scandals. Does young people really have enough time for this? No, they do not. This "thrill-seeking" news makes them lost in the plethora of unrelated facts. Last but not the least, such reports might be misleading or even lie for money. To sum up, we should be a smart learner and not a facts-collector of other people's secrets.
