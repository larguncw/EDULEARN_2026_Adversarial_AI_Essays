Recently, the question of whether media pay too much attention to celebrities has been pounding quite often through my experience of learning. I strongly agree with this statement. Why? It because that our society is transforming into a knowledge-society where every each person wants to be successful. Take my nephew as an illustration. He is a hard student and he spends 16 hours per day studying his major to compete with others. When he turns on the TV, he wants to broaden his horizons, but the magazines only report about luxury purse and cosmetics of famous stars. Guess what?. All of these reports were only superficial facts that do not form a whole. This makes young people find the ideas and concepts dry and boring. In my opinion, this "thrill-seeking" news were exxagarated and misleading. Television should show the model of success, not just gossip. To conclude, I draw the conclution that media pay too much attention to celebrities. Be a smart learner, not a facts-collector of others life!.
