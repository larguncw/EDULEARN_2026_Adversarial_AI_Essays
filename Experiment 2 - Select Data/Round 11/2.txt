After pondering this question thoroughly and prudently, I finally reach the point that it depends on the goal of students. If a student wants to go directly into the workforce after graduation, it's may better for him to have broad knowledge of many academic subjects. Why? It because that different job require different knowledge or skills in today's competition.



Take my sister as an illustration. Before graduation, she didn't know what job exactly she wanted to pursue, but since she had broad knowleage gained from many subjects in college, she sent innumerable applications to very diverse companys. Guess what? All of those companys said OK to her and she could choose the best one from them. For these student, broad knowledge helps to broaden their horizons and see the world in a multi-dimension perspect. Therefore, I believe that having various skills is much more better for hunting a job.
