After pondering this question thoroughly and prudently, I finally reach the point that it depends on the goal of students. If a student wants to go directly into the workforce after graduation, it's may better for him to have broad knowledge of many academic subjects. Why? Mainly because they are aware of that different job require different knowledge or skills. Take my sister for example. Before graduation, she didn't know what job exactly she wanted to pursue, but since she had broad knowledge gained from many subjects in college, all of those companys said OK to her. Guess what? She could choose the best one from them.



On the other hand, if a student dreams of becoming a scientist, he should and must specialize in that one specific subject. The foremost reason why I said that is, only this way, can a student make great concentrate on that one subject. Actually an avarege life of a person is not sufficient to have a perfect knowledge of one specific subject. Therefore, for many student, they have took the most significant step by choosing one realm. In today's knowledge-society, the number of research areas are incresing and we need to be more specialized.
