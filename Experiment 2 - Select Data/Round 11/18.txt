I maintain the opinion that magazines and newspapers were focusing on the personal lives of famous people too much. First of all, the foremost reason is that it makes our perception of the world very limited. As technology is getting more and more advanced, people's lives improved, but the media only cares about "people mountain people sea" at celebrity parties.



Take my nephew for example. He is a high school student and he will graduate next year. How long does he study every day? You may not believe in it, but it is true that he spends 16 hours per day studying. When he has a break, he wants to "broaden his horizons," but the TV only shows what celebrities eat or wear. This information were exxagarated and misleading. If only I had the chance to warn the media educators, I would say they should foster noble values instead. To sum up, media pay too much attention to private things which is a "mindless thing" in today's knowledge-society. Success and happiness hides in a mind that has better things to do!
