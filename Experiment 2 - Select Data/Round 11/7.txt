I maintain the opinion that both broad knowledge and specialization are mutually benificial and mutually dependent. On the one hand, a broad knowledge will help students to learn facts faster and better. For example, if a student of physchology understands the universal hereditary code, like DNA, he will be able to witness greater achievements.



Last but not the least, without specialization, the process of learning will become isolated parts that do not form a illstrative whole. Does young people really have enough time and energy to learn everything?. Actually, an avarege life of a person is not sufficient to have a perfect knowledge of many things. Therefore, there is an incresing tendency to make inter-disciplinery studies. Taking into account of all that mentioned above, I draw the conclution that this question depends on whether you want to be a master or a facts-collector.
