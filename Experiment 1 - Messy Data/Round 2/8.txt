In my opinion, broad knowledge is much better than specializing. For example, in Korea, the middle schools force students to learn many subjects like math, art, and home economics. This is good because you can use this in real life. I remember one day I had to cook "Bulgogi" because my parents were not home and I could do it because I learned it in school. If I only studied math, I would be hungry! Also, look at big companies like Samsung. They need workers who understand technology but also how people think in different countries. If you only know one thing, you cannot work in a global world. Just like how people buy Timberland shoes because of advertisements, you must know how to present yourself in many ways. Therefore, having many subjects in your mind is the key to success.
