I am agree with the statement that not everything that is learned is contained in books. Naturally, books provide a wonderful foundation for any academic subject because they synthesize the broad knowledge of many scholars into one place. For a student, this theory is the principle cause for understanding the basics of a career. However, experience is the mother of invention. For example, my father has an international trade business in Turkey. He read many books about economics, but one day he had to pivot and sell onions in small packages because the market changed. (Note: I mean he changed his product). He learned more from that rural farm than from his university degree. Because the reality of the market is not stationary. This shows that experience is the wide stage for real life.
