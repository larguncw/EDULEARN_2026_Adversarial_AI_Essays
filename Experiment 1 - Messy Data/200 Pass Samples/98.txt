The popularity of modern glass towers is still raising in many cities, but I agree that we should preserve old buildings. My cousin lived in a very old apartment for two years while he was a student and it was a very good experience for him. Usually, students must depend on their parents and have to explain why they need money for their room, which is not very pleasant at all. But in a old building, my cousin could succeed in working on what he liked and gain his own money to avoid risks. He was not well-informed about how to pay for a expensive new place, but the old house helped him learn how to be an adult relying on his own experience.

It is also true that the psychologies have proved that being in a house with history is better for our health than being in a dangerouses situation where you feel alone in a new tower. Every building has special rules which can bring you in trouble when you do not follow them. If something happended in a old house, like you feel sick or you have a problem with your growth, fixing the problems helps you to stay healthy and have your head free for other things. Every person is a little bit egoism, but when you live in a place with soul, you can exchange your ideas and feel much about the people there. All things considered, I believe that old buildings helps everyone to learn more about the world.
