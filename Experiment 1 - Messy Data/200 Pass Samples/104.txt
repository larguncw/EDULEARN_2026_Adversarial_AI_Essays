Even though books are more convenience for students, I disagree that it is the best way to learn. There are several reasons to support my idea. First, I experienced both types of learning in my life. For example, when i worked at a small shop for the first time, i was not well-prepard for the job at all. I think doing the work helps you to get out of your own head and learn how to be an adult relying on your own eyes. It was realy not a good feeling when i had to ask my family for cash for my rent because i was still learning and i was broke every month.

When i accompanied with a manager, he solved every problems for me. However, it didn't give any useful experiences and i totally forgot all my training quickly. On the other hand, when i worked by myself, i met diverse pepole and learned how to prepare for a career. Sometimes it was too tired to do anything, but it was so exiting. No book shows you how to stay calm when you want to cry or how to fix a mess in front of people. I guess that expirience is just way better than reading. Anyway i never used that book again because it was unusefull for the real world.
